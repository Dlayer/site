The history of Dlayer How it came to be

As of August 2015 Dlayer is an Open Source project, the text below is from the original history page on Dlayer.com.

The idea When was the lightbulb moment?

Thinking back I believe the initial idea for Dlayer solidified in early 2008, I can't pin down the exact date that I settled on the final idea but I do know that I had been thinking about the concept, either in parts or as a whole for at least a few years prior to 2008.

I've spent a lot of time thinking about why it took so long for the idea to cement and I believe now that before 2008 I wasn't completely sure of my abilities, for the majority of the previous decade I had either been working by myself or in very small teams with only junior members, I didn't have any peers to compare myself to. I started working in larger and more skilled teams after 2008 and became very conscious I had artificially limited some of my early development projects because I thought I wasn't able to complete them on my own or I was lacking certain skills.

The Dlayer that never was Wistful thinking!

There was nearly a vaguely similar product to Dlayer in 2008, as in I nearly started working on a product with two other colleagues from work. At the time I was Head of Development at a small web development company in Newbury, UK, I would have been working on the product with one of the Product Managers, lets call him Foo and the Lead Designer, lets call him Bar.

During a carpool drive home with Bar we talked about working on a project together, the plan was that he would do all the creative work and I would do all the development work, Foo was going to handle commercial and marketing.

Before I continue with the story it needs to be stated that the proposed product was not Dlayer and it would never have been able to become Dlayer, it was a very simple template driven system. Bar was going to create thousands or small image and HTML assets and I was going to develop a system that would allow users to join them all together to create whatever they wanted.

Although on the surface it could be argued that this product and Dlayer are similar the reality is they couldn't be any more different if they tried, it would be like attempting to argue that a unicycle and Mercedes SLK are the same, oh my god, a car analogy. The two products differed both conceptually and architecturally, on top of that, Dlayer exists and is on the web, this imagined other product never even got to the planning stage.

Why am I mentioning the other potential product here? Three reasons, firstly, full disclosure, I've no reason to hide anything, secondly, I know there is an link between the two products and never was, lastly, this all happened around the time I started working on Dlayer, 2008.

Very boring and long story short, nothing happened, neither Foo or Bar were willing to commit to the product, even partially, it was all just a wistful dream.

Delayed start Procrastination?

Between 2008 and early 2010 there was very little real development on what would later become the product you are reading about. During the mentioned time frame I wrote thousands of lines of code, mainly samples, endless documentation and created hundreds of diagrams detailing how I intended the app to work, however, very little real code.

There are a multitude of reasons for the lack of development, two of the major reasons being that I met my wife to be and I moved over to contracting rather than full time development roles, time just managed to get away from me.

Development begins Finally!

In early 2010 I realised how long I had been sitting on the concept for Dlayer and how very little I had to show for two years of work. I spent the next few months collating all my documentation and started planning the development of Dlayer. Development began in earnest during September, I had finally started development on the first real version of the product that would later become the concept that is currently hosted at Dlayer.com.

Over the next 21 months I created two separate versions of Dlayer, they both stalled at exactly the same point, I kept stumbling with how I wanted to handle the relationship between a content page and a content page template.

Hindsight is a wonderful thing, I had got far too close to the project, it took a meeting with my best friend, lets call him Dan to realise that. I let Dan have a play with the latest version and after a very long postmortem I realised my error, Dlayer was far too complicated, I had strayed from my original concept and primary goal, a user shouldn't need to understand web design or web development, Dlayer was at that point a tool only really usable by web developers.

I gave myself a rather large kick in the arse and went back to my original notes and planned development for yet another version of Dlayer, third times a charm?

I started the development of the current version on the 1st May 2012, the current specification refers to this version and it very much mirrors the original concept I started outlining in 2008.
